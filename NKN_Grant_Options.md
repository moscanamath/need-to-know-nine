# Grant options for Need to Know Nine — research summary
*(as of July 15, 2026 — verify current deadlines before drafting; NSF/ED timelines shift)*

## 1. NSF STEM K-12 (NSF 25-545) — strongest near-term fit
- **Who it's for:** Research on STEM teaching and learning, K-12, run out of NSF's Directorate for STEM Education (DRL division).
- **Money:** ~40 awards/year, ~$30M total pool; most awards $25,000–$750,000, 1–3 years.
- **Deadline:** Accepted **anytime**, except a blackout window of April 15–June 15 each year. Right now (mid-July) the window is open.
- **Why it fits:** NKN isn't just a product, it's a testable hypothesis — does daily spiral review across 9 categories improve long-term retention and DOK-level progression versus traditional sequential units? NSF STEM K-12 funds exactly this kind of applied/translational research, and 14 years of your own classroom data is a real head start on a research design.
- **Catch:** This is a research grant, not a product grant. The proposal needs a research question, a study design (likely a pilot across a few classrooms/grades), and measurable outcomes — not just "help us build the app."
- Solicitation: https://www.nsf.gov/funding/opportunities/stem-k-12-nsf-stem-k-12/nsf25-545/solicitation

## 2. NSF SBIR/STTR — Learning and Cognition Technologies topic — UPDATED: you qualify
- **Who it's for:** Small businesses (under 500 employees, majority US-owned) building novel ed/workforce tech. AI-in-education is explicitly listed as an area of interest.
- **Money:** Up to $2M in seed funding, **zero equity taken**.
- **Deadline:** Rolling "Project Pitch" process — no fixed annual deadline, but requires going through their pitch/assessment stages first.
- **Why it fits:** NKN's digital app (AI-generated problems, AI handwriting grading, adaptive difficulty) is a legitimate ed-tech product, not just a research artifact — this track funds the product itself, and it's specifically for building/scaling what you already have.
- **Now that you've confirmed MOSCANA Mathematics LLC exists**, the earlier blocker (needing a formal business entity) is resolved. Two things to confirm before applying: (1) fewer than 500 employees and majority US-citizen/permanent-resident owned — almost certainly yes for a single-owner LLC; (2) you (or a designated technical lead) can commit ~20 hrs/week and legally be employed by the LLC for the project — worth thinking through given your full-time Chariho role.
- This moves from a "2027 consideration" to a **real near-term option**, possibly the strongest one since it funds the product directly.
- Topic page: https://seedfund.nsf.gov/topics/learning-cognition-technologies/

## 3. ED/IES SBIR (Dept. of Education, Institute of Education Sciences)
- **Who it's for:** Ed-tech companies building classroom-ready prototypes — explicitly funds AI adaptive tutors as a named category.
- **Money:** Phase I ~$250,000/9 months; Phase II ~$1,000,000/2 years.
- **Deadline:** The 2026 cycle solicitation was expected to release ~April 30, 2026, with proposals due ~60 days later (roughly late June 2026) — **that window has likely already closed** for this year. Worth confirming directly with IES, but realistically this is a 2027-cycle target.
- Program page: https://ies.ed.gov/funding/research/programs/small-business-innovation-research-sbir
